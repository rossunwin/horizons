About Us

We are providing a new solution to the hospitality industry to meet the growing demand for quality gin drinks.

GIN

Gin is Britain's most popular alcoholic drink. A huge market exists that most bars are not tapping into, and teh main reason for this is most bars only offer a very basic London dry gin and post-mix tonic. So we wanted to solve this.

FLAVOURS

Most bars at the moment have a house gin and tonic that they sell to most customers asking for a G&T. The customer has to spend a considerable amount more to order a custom (and better tasting) G&T. We believe that this problem can be solved with the addition of carefully selected flavours.

SPEED

Mixologists like to put on a show when making cocktails. However, this hugely slows down the process, and can make a gin cocktail take in excess of 5 minutes to makes. When a bar wants high throughput, we are the solution.

Tools used for website design

To create this website, I used a bootstrap template. This gave me the basic html, javascript and css for the website. Steps taken to register and setup domain

One of the most important aspects in setting up a domain name is the name itself. This needs to be memorable and creative, and more importantly available. Services like GoDaddy.com and 123-reg.co.uk allow you to search for available domain names and purchase them. These sites allow you to register an account and pay monthly or annually for your chosen domain. This is very simple and easy to do, and payment can be made by debit/credit card online. The CNAME record of this new domain name needs to be edited to point to the address where the website is hosted. A web hosting service is also required to connect the site to the internet (we are currently using Git) but without web hosting, the website will not be visible on the internet. To ensure Internet security, a cryptographic protocol such as TLS certificate should be obtained. This ensure that data is transferred safely and creates a secure encrypted connection between browsers and our server.

Website

What makes a good website

A good website should load quickly and be easy to navigate through. In terms of loading time, people will often leave a site if it has not loaded within 2 seconds – this is hugely important as it means if your website loads slowly, people will never look at your content. In terms of ease of navigation, every page should link to other pages meaning that the user is never stuck at a dead end, the content in the website should also be ordered in a fashion that makes sense and carries the user through a story. The aesthetics are also hugely important, as this conveys your brand to the user. And lastly, a good website should be viewable on the majority of devices in existence, allowing users access to your content regardless of their screen size.

Accessibility

Well designed websites are designed in a way that enables people with disabilities to use them, this is accessibility and there are international standards that define what is needed. This means that accessibility is crucial for websites so as not to exclude people from using them. To make a website accessible, people must be able to perceive, understand, navigate and interact with it, regardless of their disability. These disabilities may include, audio, visual, physical, neurological and cognitive impairments that limit how easy it is for someone to interact with a page. To make this website accessible, I ran it through webaccessibility.com, and tried to adhere to best practice such as ensuring link text is meaningful within context.

GDPR

GDPR stands for General Data Protection Regulation and this is the EU’s new data protection laws which came into effect on May 25 2018. Companies are accountable for the way they handle people’s personal information and there are strict regulations that they must adhere to. Personal information includes (but is not limited to) names, address, & IP address. Companies must ensure they know what personal information they are collecting, and have valid reason for collecting such data. They must also notify users what personal information they are using and how this will be processed (for most websites, this is done via a pop up). One of the largest elements of GDPR is that companies can now be fined by regulators if they don’t comply with GDPR rules. For this website, a pop up could be installed so that users must accept the collection of their personal data in line with GDPR, or the website could collect no personal information.

Flavour Selection

How is your flavour selected?

We have come up with the innovatiove solution of using a carousel of flavours. The final design will house over 20 syringes allowing the user to pick from over 1 million flavour combinations. A stepper motor and some clever software has been used to optimise the rotation of the carousel and move between required flavours in teh fastest way possible.

Dispensing

How do we ensure the correct amoutn of flavouring goes into your glass?

Every flavour combo will need a slightly different amount of varying flavours. Oils, extracts, essences, and syrups also all require different amounts of liquid dispensed. To solve this issue, the on board chip will know which flavour is in which location, and a the syringe will be depressed the correct amount accordingly. This depression is achived through the use of a simple linear actuator, which is controlled in a similar way to the flavour selection. SOme clever optimisation has also been utilised here which means that the plunger will never collide with any syringes, and yet will never travel more distance than required.

Pumping

How do we get the perfect serve of gin and tonic every time?

One of the problems bars encounter is slow and incorrect measurement of spirits and mixers into the glass. Most bars use a simple 25ml/50ml measure to get the amount of spirit correct, but bartenders are known to over/under pour, and spillage occurs. WHen it comes to the mixer side of things, the amount of mixer is always different, and is often just used to top up a glass after the spirit and a random amount of ice have been added. We solev this by using peristaltic pumps that correctly dispense the right amount of gin and mixer every single time. Our pumps are also used to force our liquids through a heat exchanger which cools your drink down to the perfcet temperature and gets rid of the need for ice. This saves bars time and money and will provide the customer with the same, high quality, undiluted, perfectly chilled drink every single time!

Ease of Use

How will the bartender and customer interact with the machine?

The ginfusion allows the bartender to press one button and let the drink dispense whilst they focus on their interaction with the customer. This allows for a much more personal, customised and enjoyable interaction between the bartender and customer. FOr the customer, they'll be able to select whatever combination of flavours they desire (guided by an expertly crafted menu for each bar) and enjoy a quickly dispensed, perfect drink.

Flavours

Is 1 million flavour combinations too much?

The ginfusion machine has the capabiltiy of holding 20 different flavours. These 20 can be selected from a roster of over 50 different flavours. We will suggest a standard 10 (the most popular flavours) to every bar that has one of our machines, and then each bar can select 10 of their own flavours to suit the specific target market they attract. These flavours will be dispatched in accordance with expected demand. The bartender will be able to refill syringes from flavour packs, thus reducing plastci waste and protecting the planet.

Final Concept

What will this look like in a bar?

The vision is for the machine to be housed in 3 seperate segments on the bar top. One to house the gin and tonic, the middle section to house the cooling system, and the final section to house the flavour selector and dispenser. It will eventually be made of hammered copper and glass pipe. And the customer will be able to see the flow of fluid between each section, and possibel cutouts in the hammered copper will give them a window into the internal workings of each section. Bars will be able to customise this experience with the addition of lighting fixtures. Either led strips that light up according to progress of drink through dispensing and change according to flavour; or swivel spotlights that light up sections accordingly.
